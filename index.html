Detailed Mixtures & Construction Guide

Black Powder Mixtures

1. Maximum Power

  • Potassium Nitrate: 77%
  • Charcoal: 13%
  • Sulfur: 10%
  • Fast ignition, high energy, short burn.

2. Longer Burn

  • Potassium Nitrate: 70%
  • Charcoal: 20%
  • Sulfur: 10%
  • Moderate ignition, steady sparks.

Sugar Rocket Fuel Mixtures

1. Maximum Power

  • Potassium Nitrate: 70%
  • Sugar: 25%
  • Aluminum Powder: 5%
  • High thrust, short burn duration.

2. Longer Burn

  • Potassium Nitrate: 60%
  • Sugar: 40%
  • Easier ignition, slower, longer-lasting burn.

Construction Guides

Rocket Construction

  • Use cardboard tube or PVC (schedule 40 recommended).
  • Insert clay plug and nozzle; drill a small hole for exhaust.
  • Pack fuel firmly, drill a core hole for thrust.
  • Add fuse or igniter, and fins for stability.

Popper Construction (PVC)

  • Use short thin-wall PVC pipe (3â€“6 inches).
  • Secure one end cap firmly.
  • Add fuel loosely to moderately packed.
  • Drill a small hole in other end cap, insert fuse or igniter.
  • Lightly secure cap to allow rapid pressure buildup.

Safety: Always ignite remotely, wear protective gear, and use thin-wall PVC to avoid dangerous fragmentation.

Smoke Grenade Construction (Potassium Nitrate)

  • Mixture: 60% Potassium Nitrate, 40% Sugar.
  • Heat slowly until caramel-colored paste forms, then pour into cardboard tube or can.
  • Insert fuse before hardening fully.
  • Add baking soda (~2%) for denser smoke (optional).
  • Ignite outdoors and maintain safety precautions.

Safety Precautions

  • Use protective equipment (gloves, goggles, mask).
  • Store materials safely, away from ignition sources.
  • Always test small quantities first.

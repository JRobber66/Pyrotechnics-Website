Detailed Mixtures & Construction Guide

Black Powder Mixtures

1. Maximum Power

  • Potassium Nitrate: 77%
  • Charcoal: 13%
  • Sulfur: 10%
  • Fast ignition, high energy, short burn.

2. Longer Burn

  • Potassium Nitrate: 70%
  • Charcoal: 20%
  • Sulfur: 10%
  • Moderate ignition, steady sparks.

Sugar Rocket Fuel Mixtures

1. Maximum Power

  • Potassium Nitrate: 70%
  • Sugar: 25%
  • Aluminum Powder: 5%
  • High thrust, short burn duration.

2. Longer Burn

  • Potassium Nitrate: 60%
  • Sugar: 40%
  • Easier ignition, slower, longer-lasting burn.

Flash Powder (Aluminum-Based)

1. Maximum Power (Rapid Ignition, Loudest Report)

  • Potassium Perchlorate: 70%
  • Aluminum Powder (fine mesh): 30%
  • Very fast ignition, extremely bright flash, very loud explosion.

2. Longer Burn (Slightly Slower Flash)

  • Potassium Nitrate: 55%
  • Aluminum Powder: 35%
  • Sulfur: 10%
  • Bright flash with slightly longer burn, moderate sensitivity.

Highly sensitive and dangerous—handle with extreme caution.

Strobe Firework Mixtures

  • Potassium Nitrate or Potassium Perchlorate: ~50%
  • Sulfur: ~30%
  • Fine Magnesium or Aluminum Powder: ~20%
  • Creates intermittent bright flashes due to controlled combustion.
  • Insert mixture into small cardboard tube, use fuse or igniter.
  • Sensitive mixture, handle gently and ignite outdoors.

Construction Guides

Rocket Construction

  • Use cardboard tube or PVC (schedule 40 recommended).
  • Insert clay plug and nozzle; drill a small hole for exhaust.
  • Pack fuel firmly, drill a core hole for thrust.
  • Add fuse or igniter, and fins for stability.

Firecracker Construction (PVC)

  • Use short thin-wall PVC pipe (3–6 inches).
  • Secure one end cap firmly.
  • Add fuel loosely to moderately packed.
  • Drill a small hole in other end cap, insert fuse or igniter.
  • Lightly secure cap to allow rapid pressure buildup.

Safety: Use thin-wall PVC to avoid dangerous fragmentation.

Smoke Grenade Construction (Potassium Nitrate)

1. Maximum Smoke Volume

  • Potassium Nitrate: 55%
  • Sugar: 45%
  • Baking soda (~2%) optional for denser smoke.

2. Longer Smoke Duration

  • Potassium Nitrate: 65%
  • Sugar: 35%
  • Produces steady, prolonged smoke.
  • Heat slowly until caramel-colored paste forms, then pour into cardboard tube or can.
  • Insert fuse before hardening fully.

Safety Precautions

  • Always test small quantities first.
  • Store materials safely, away from ignition sources.
  • Don't be irresponsible—handle all materials carefully.

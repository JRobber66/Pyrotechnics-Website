Experiment 2 — Extended Boiling Time Study

Mixture & Preparation

  • 1 cup water
  • Approximately 1 1/2 test parts Potassium Nitrate (KNO₃)

Burn Test Results by Boil Time

3-Minute Boil

  • Burn times: 2:40, 2:18
  • Average: 2:29 ± 0:11

4-Minute Boil

  • Burn times: 2:58, 2:35, 2:41
  • Average: 2:45 ± 0:13

5-Minute Boil

  • Burn times: 1:28, 2:19, 1:48
  • Average: 1:52 ± 0:26

7-Minute Boil

  • Burn times: 52s, 58s, 55s
  • Average: 55s ± 3s

8-Minute Boil

  • Burn times: 1:30, 1:09, 2:08
  • Average: 1:35 ± 29s

9-Minute Boil

  • Note: Strings were flexed to loosen fibers ("broken in")
  • Burn time: 1:13

12-Minute Boil

  • Note: A different dyed string was used
  • All strings from here on were broken in
  • Burn times: 1:03, 46s, 33s
  • Average: 47s ± 15s

13-Minute Boil

  • Burn times: 31s, 43s, 30s
  • Average: 35s ± 7s

14-Minute Boil

  • Burn times: 12s, 13s, 17s
  • Average: 14s ± 3s

15-Minute Boil

  • Initial burn: 5 seconds (stopped)
  • Relit and continued for 12 more seconds

Additional Experiment

  • Two 15-minute boiled fuses were wrapped together
  • Burned aggressively for 14 seconds

Separate Observation: High Saturation Test

  • Mixture was highly saturated
  • Failed to maintain flame once ignition source was removed, even when bundled

Conclusion

  • Breaking in string may reduce burn time and increase fuse reliability
  • Highly saturated strings lose combustion stability
